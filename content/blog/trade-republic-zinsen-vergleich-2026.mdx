Das ist kein Vorwurf an Trade Republic. Es ist die systemimmanente Grenze des EZB-Geldmarkts.

## Risiken sachlich betrachtet: Was Anleger wissen müssen

Kein seriöser Vergleich kommt ohne Risikoanalyse aus. Beide Plattformen tragen spezifische Risiken — sie sind nur anderer Natur.

**Risiken bei Trade Republic:**

1. **Zinssatzrisiko:** Der Tagesgeldszins ist variabel und an EZB-Entscheidungen gebunden. Bei weiteren Zinssenkungen kann die Rendite auf 3% oder darunter fallen.
2. **Inflationsrisiko:** Bei anhaltender Kerninflation über 2% wird die Realrendite negativ — Kaufkraftverlust trotz nominaler Verzinsung.
3. **Konzentrationsrisiko:** Wer mehr als €100.000 bei Trade Republic hält, ist über der EU-Einlagensicherungsgrenze ungesichert.

**Risiken bei Rentiers Pro:**

1. **Währungsrisiko:** GEL, AMD oder TRY können gegenüber dem Euro erheblich abwerten. Die [BIS-Statistiken](https://www.bis.org/statistics/) dokumentieren die historische Volatilität dieser Währungen. EUR-Hedging reduziert, eliminiert dieses Risiko aber nicht vollständig.
2. **Länderrisiko:** Politische oder wirtschaftliche Instabilität im Zielland kann die Einlagengarantie gefährden. Staatliche Garantiesysteme in Schwellenländern sind nicht mit dem EU-EDIS vergleichbar.
3. **Liquiditätsrisiko:** Einlagen sind laufzeitgebunden. Frühauflösung ist je nach Vertrag nicht oder nur mit Strafzinsen möglich.
4. **Regulierungsrahmen:** Rentiers Pro ist als MSB bei FinCEN und FINTRAC registriert — kein EU-reguliertes Institut. Anleger haben nicht die gleichen Schutzrechte wie bei einem deutschen Kreditinstitut.
5. **Komplexität:** Steuerliche Behandlung ausländischer Zinserträge, Währungsgewinne und Quellensteueranrechnung erfordern fachkundige steuerliche Beratung.

Die Frage [ob Einlagenarbitrage legal ist](/blog/einlagenarbitrage-erklaert-legal) wird häufig gestellt: Das Prinzip ist legal; entscheidend ist die korrekte steuerliche Deklaration in Deutschland.

## The Starting Point: What Trade Republic Actually Pays in 2026

Trade Republic is not a traditional credit institution but a regulated neo-broker holding a banking licence from the Bundesbank. Its cash deposit offering — frequently discussed under the term "Trade Republic fixed deposit", though technically it functions as interest-bearing cash — is directly linked to the ECB deposit facility rate.

Following a series of ECB rate cuts since mid-2024, the relevant ECB rate stands at approximately 2.5–2.75% in early 2026. Trade Republic currently pays up to 4% p.a. on balances up to €50,000, with tiered rates applying above that threshold. Critically, this rate is **variable**. Should the ECB cut rates further, Trade Republic's yield will follow immediately.

The [Bundesbank documents](https://www.bundesbank.de/de/statistiken/preise) core inflation across the eurozone stabilising at 2.2–2.4% through 2025. At a 4% nominal rate, that leaves a real yield of roughly 1.6–1.8% — before German withholding tax (Abgeltungsteuer at 26.375% including solidarity surcharge). After tax, real returns shrink to below 1%.

This is not a criticism of Trade Republic. It is the systemic ceiling of ECB money market conditions.

## What Rentiers Pro Does Differently: The Deposit Arbitrage Principle

Rentiers Pro is neither a bank nor a broker. It is a technology platform that makes deposit arbitrage — the strategic placement of capital in high-yield banking markets — accessible to European private investors.

The core principle: banks in emerging markets such as Georgia and Armenia pay high deposit rates because their central banks are combating inflation or managing capital flows. The [National Bank of Georgia](https://nbg.gov.ge) has maintained its policy rate between 9.5–11% since 2023, enabling commercial banks such as TBC Bank and Bank of Georgia to offer GEL-denominated deposits at 12–14% p.a. In Armenia, comparable rates sit at 10–13% according to the [Central Bank of Armenia](https://www.cba.am).

Rentiers Pro bundles access to these markets, manages the legal and administrative complexity, and charges a service fee of **25% of the gross yield** in return. Net yield is therefore calculated as: bank interest rate × 0.75.

**Example:** 20% gross yield in local currency → 15% net yield for the investor — in local currency, before hedging.

How much of this translates into euros depends on exchange rate movements or the cost of optional EUR hedging. This is addressed in detail in the risk section below.

## Risks Assessed Objectively: What Investors Need to Know

No credible comparison is complete without a rigorous risk analysis. Both platforms carry specific risks — they simply differ in nature.

**Risks with Trade Republic:**

1. **Interest rate risk:** The cash yield is variable and follows ECB decisions. Further rate cuts could push returns below 3%.

3. **Concentration risk:** Balances above €100,000 are unprotected by EU deposit insurance.

**Risks with Rentiers Pro:**

1. **Currency risk:** GEL, AMD, or TRY can depreciate substantially against the euro. [BIS statistics](https://www.bis.org/statistics/) document the historical volatility of these currencies. EUR hedging reduces but does not eliminate this risk.
2. **Country risk:** Political or economic instability in the host country can impair the deposit guarantee. State guarantee schemes in emerging markets are not equivalent to the EU's EDIS framework.
3. **Liquidity risk:** Deposits are term-locked. Early redemption may not be possible, or may incur penalty charges.
4. **Regulatory framework:** Rentiers Pro is registered as a Money Services Business with FinCEN and FINTRAC — not an EU-regulated institution. Investors do not hold the same statutory protections as with a German credit institution.
5. **Complexity:** The tax treatment of foreign interest income, currency gains, and foreign withholding tax credits requires professional tax advice in the investor's home country.
